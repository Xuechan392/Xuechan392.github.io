Current & Recent Work

● Active · Pilot Study

Tone Sandhi in Guiyang Mandarin

UVA LING 5410 · 2025 · Supervisor: Armik Mirzayan

An acoustic investigation of citation tones and AA-reduplication sandhi patterns in Guiyang Mandarin, a Southwest Mandarin variety spoken by ~4 million people and largely absent from the phonological literature.

Key finding: sandhi in kinship-term reduplication appears sensitive to semantic context — the same surface form 婆婆 surfaces with distinct tone patterns depending on whether it denotes 'paternal grandmother' or 'mother-in-law'.

Interests

What I Think About

01

Tonal Phonology

How do tones behave in context? What triggers sandhi, and what does that tell us about phonological representation?

02

Syntax–Phonology Interface

The boundaries between words, phrases, and clauses shape how sounds are realized. I want to understand exactly how.

03

Understudied Varieties

Southwest Mandarin dialects and Guizhou minority languages are rich testing grounds for phonological theory. They deserve more attention.

04

Language Acquisition

How do learners parse tonal contrasts in an L2? What does processing difficulty reveal about phonological categories?

05

Acoustic Methods

Praat, Python, and the painstaking art of converting continuous F0 into meaningful phonological description.

06

Language & Identity

Accent prejudice is real. Linguistics can be a tool for understanding — and challenging — how we judge speakers.
