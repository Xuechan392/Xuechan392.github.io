Projects & Interests

My work sits at the intersection of phonology, the syntax–phonology interface, and the acoustic study of tonal varieties — with a particular focus on Southwest Mandarin dialects and other understudied varieties of Chinese.

Primary Project
● Active · Pilot Study · 2025–present

Tone Sandhi in Guiyang Mandarin

UVA LING 5410 Phonology · Supervisor: Armik Mirzayan · Writing sample available

Guiyang Mandarin is a Southwest Mandarin variety spoken by approximately 4 million people in Guiyang, Guizhou Province. Despite its size, it remains largely absent from the phonological literature. This project offers the first acoustic investigation of its citation tones and tone sandhi patterns in AA-reduplication — a structure used extensively in kinship terminology.

Key Finding

Sandhi in kinship-term reduplication appears sensitive to semantic context. The form 婆婆 surfaces with distinct tone patterns depending on whether it denotes 'paternal grandmother' or 'mother-in-law' — suggesting that sandhi here may be conditioned by morphological or semantic factors, not phonology alone.

Data were collected from a single speaker (the author) using iPhone recording in a quiet indoor environment. F0 was extracted using Praat and the parselmouth Python library, and converted to five-point tone values using Shi Feng's T-value method. The pilot study documents citation tones and 19 kinship-term AA-reduplication forms.

This study provides a preliminary acoustic description of tonal phenomena in Guiyang Mandarin, a Southwest Mandarin variety spoken in Guizhou Province, China. Using data collected from a single native speaker, I document the citation tone values of the four lexical tones (T1: 35, T2: 31, T3: 44, T4: 14) and examine tone sandhi patterns in AA-reduplication of kinship terms. Analysis reveals that while A1 (the first syllable) remains relatively stable across reduplication contexts, A2 (the second syllable) undergoes dramatic shifts. Crucially, some reduplicated forms — notably 婆婆 — exhibit distinct sandhi patterns depending on their semantic referent, suggesting that sandhi conditioning in Guiyang Mandarin may extend beyond purely phonological domains. These findings point toward morpho-phonological or semantic conditioning of sandhi and motivate future work with a larger speaker sample and broader range of syntactic structures.

Citation Tones

Four tones documented

T1: 35 (rising) · T2: 31 (falling) · T3: 44 (high-level) · T4: 14 (low-falling). T3 pattern deviates from expected falling-rising contour.

Sandhi Patterns

Semantic conditioning

婆婆 and 弟弟 each surface with distinct sandhi patterns depending on semantic referent — a pattern not predicted by purely phonological accounts.

Methodology

Acoustic + computational

Praat TextGrid annotation, Python F0 extraction via parselmouth, T-value conversion for five-point tone representation.

Limitation

Single-speaker pilot

Current data cannot distinguish systematic patterns from idiolectal variation. Expanded fieldwork with 10+ speakers planned for Summer 2026.

Interests
Research Directions

Tonal Phonology

Citation tones, sandhi environments, and the conditioning of tonal alternations — with focus on Chinese varieties.

Syntax–Phonology Interface

How syntactic structure and prosodic constituency interact to shape phonological output, especially in tone sandhi domains.

Understudied Varieties

Southwest Mandarin dialects and Guizhou minority languages as testing grounds for phonological theory.

Experimental & Acoustic Methods

F0 extraction, T-value methods, and the challenges of converting continuous pitch data to discrete tonal representations.

Psycholinguistics

Real-time processing of tonal contrasts in L1 and L2 Mandarin; what processing difficulty reveals about phonological categories.

Language Documentation

Fieldwork methodology for under-documented varieties; building phonological descriptions from the ground up.
